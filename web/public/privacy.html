Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 16, 2026

Dreamcatcher is a personal job-search tool that reads the job-alert emails you already receive from job boards, pulls out the individual listings, checks them for signs of scams, and organizes them so you can review and apply more easily. This policy explains exactly what data the app accesses, how it is used, and — just as importantly — what it never does with your information.

The Google data we access

When you sign in with Google, Dreamcatcher requests a single permission: read-only access to your Gmail (the gmail.readonly scope). We use this access solely to find and read the job-alert emails sent by job boards such as LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor, ZipRecruiter, and Monster.

  • Read-only. The app can only view messages. It can never send, reply to, delete, label, or modify anything in your mailbox.
  • Limited to job alerts. Every time it syncs, the app queries Gmail for messages from a specific list of job-alert sender addresses. Emails from anyone else — friends, family, banks, work — are never requested and never seen by the app.
  • Why this scope. Reading the message body is necessary because the job title, company, location, and application link live inside the body of the alert email. Narrower Gmail scopes that expose only headers or metadata do not contain this information, so they cannot power the app's core feature.

How we use it

When an alert email is read, the app extracts a small amount of public, non-personal information about each job listing:

  • Job title, company, and location
  • Pay range, when the listing includes one
  • The public link to the job posting

This information is used to display your job listings, score each one for scam risk, remove duplicates, and flag postings that are repeatedly reposted. It is shown only to you, inside your own account.

What we store — and what we don't

Your emails are never stored. After the app reads an alert email to extract the job details above, the email itself is discarded from memory. The subject line, the body text, the sender, and every other message are never written to our database or saved anywhere.

The only things saved to the server are:

  • The extracted job details listed above (title, company, location, pay, link).
  • The Gmail message ID — an opaque identifier, not the email's contents — used only to avoid showing you the same listing twice and to count reposts.
  • Your basic Google profile (email address and name) and the access token that lets the app fetch your alerts. The token is used only to talk to Gmail on your behalf and is removed when you disconnect.

What we never do

  • We never sell, rent, or share your data with anyone.
  • We never use your data for advertising.
  • We never use your data to train machine-learning or AI models.
  • No human — including the operator of Dreamcatcher — ever reads, opens, or browses your email.
  • We transfer your Google data to no third parties.

Limited Use disclosure

Dreamcatcher's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs to any other app will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Retention and deleting your data

You can revoke Dreamcatcher's access at any time from your Google Account permissions page. Signing out of the app clears your session. To have your stored job data and profile deleted from the server, contact us at the address below and it will be removed.

Security

Data is transmitted over encrypted (HTTPS) connections and stored on a private server. Because no email contents are ever stored, the amount of your data at rest is intentionally kept to a minimum.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "last updated" date above will be revised. Material changes affecting how your Google data is handled will be reflected here before they take effect.
