Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 18, 2026

Short version: Dreamcatcher reads the job-alert emails you already get from job boards, pulls out the listings, flags likely scams, and lines them up so applying is less painful. It never saves your emails, no human ever reads them, and you can download or delete everything at any time. The whole thing is open source, so you don't have to take my word for any of this — you can read the code.

Giving an app access to your email is a big deal, and you're right to be careful. Here's exactly what Dreamcatcher does and doesn't do, in plain terms.

What it can see

When you sign in with Google, Dreamcatcher asks for one thing: read-only access to your Gmail. That's the only permission.

  • It can only read — never touch. Google won't let it send, reply, delete, label, or change anything in your mailbox. Not even by accident.
  • It only opens your job alerts. Every sync searches your mail for messages from a fixed list of job-alert senders (LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor, ZipRecruiter, Monster, and any you add). The app is built so it can only search by sender — it literally can't go rummaging through the rest of your inbox. Emails from friends, family, your bank, or work are never requested and never seen.
  • Why it needs to read the body. The job title, company, and link live inside the email itself, so read access is the only way to pull them out. There isn't a narrower Google permission that would still work.

What it keeps

Your emails are never saved. Once it reads an alert and grabs the job details, the copy of the email is thrown away. The subject, the body, the sender — none of it is ever written down or stored anywhere.

Here's the short list of what actually gets saved on the server:

  • The job details it found: title, company, location, pay, and the link.
  • A Gmail message ID — a meaningless string of characters, not the email's contents — just so you don't see the same job twice.
  • Your Google name and email, so you can sign in, plus the access token that lets the app fetch your alerts. The token is encrypted on the server and is deleted the moment you disconnect.

Jobs you skip are automatically forgotten after 60 days. And if you disconnect, everything above is deleted right away.

What it never does

  • Never sells, rents, or shares your data with anyone.
  • Never uses your data for ads.
  • Never uses your data to train AI models.
  • Never lets a human — me included — read, open, or browse your email.
  • Never sends your Google data to any third party.
  • No trackers, no analytics, no ad networks. None.

You're in control

Anytime, from Settings inside the app, you can:

  • Download everything the server has on you, as a plain file.
  • Disconnect and delete everything — this wipes your data from the server and signs you out.

You can also cut off access from the Google side anytime, on your Google Account permissions page.

Don't take my word for it

Dreamcatcher is open source. If you (or a friend who codes) want to check that any of this is true, you can read the exact code that runs. You can even run your own copy on your own machine, so your data never touches my server at all.

One honest note: on the version I host, I run the server, so technically I have access to it the way anyone running a website does. I never look at your data — but if that's not good enough for you, self-hosting is the answer, and I'd rather tell you that than pretend otherwise.

The required legal bit

Dreamcatcher's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs to any other app will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Security

Everything moves over encrypted (HTTPS) connections, tokens are encrypted at rest, and because no email contents are ever stored, there's very little data sitting around in the first place. Found a security issue? Please tell me at the email below.

If this ever changes

If the policy changes, I'll update the date at the top. Anything that actually affects how your data is handled will be reflected here before it takes effect.
